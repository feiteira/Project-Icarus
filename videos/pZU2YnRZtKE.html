Before that, the global financial system worked under something called the Breton Woods Agreement. Under that framework, the US dollar had a very specific guarantee attached to it. Every dollar in circulation was backed by gold. Any country holding dollars could exchange them for actual physical gold stored in American vaults. That's what gave the dollar its credibility. That's why the world trusted it. It wasn't just paper. It was a claim on something real. But then America got into serious financial trouble. The Vietnam War was extraordinarily expensive. The government was spending money it didn't have, printing dollars to cover the gap. And slowly other countries started doing the math. They started realizing there were far more dollars floating around the world than there was gold to back them up. France famously started sending ships across the Atlantic to exchange dollars for gold before the whole thing unraveled. The game was up and in 1971, President Nixon made it official. He closed what was called the gold window. The dollar was no longer convertible to gold. Just like that, the anchor was gone. Now, think about what that moment actually meant. The dollar was now just paper. It was backed by nothing except faith in the American government. Why would the rest of the world continue to hold dollars? Why would countries price their goods in dollars? Why would international trade continue to be conducted in a currency that no longer had any physical backing? America had a serious problem, a potentially catastrophic problem. Because if global demand for dollars collapsed, the American economy would collapse along with it. So Washington went looking for a solution and they found one in the most important commodity on the planet. The United States approached Saudi Arabia and they made a deal that would quietly reshape the entire world order. The terms were straightforward. Saudi Arabia would agree to sell its oil exclusively in US dollars. Every barrel to every buyer anywhere in the world will be priced and paid for in dollars. Not pounds, not Deutsch marks, not any other currency, dollars only. And in exchange, America would guarantee the survival of the Saudi regime. Military protection, weapons sales, political cover, whatever the royal family needed to stay in power, America would provide. The Saudis agreed. Every modern economy runs on energy. Every factory, every car, every power grid, every shipping vessel. Without oil, the entire industrial world stops functioning. And in the 1970s, Saudi Arabia and its Gulf neighbors controlled an enormous share of global oil production.
