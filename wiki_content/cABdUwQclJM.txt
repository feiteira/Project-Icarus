== Overview ==

So, let me tell you something that most people get completely wrong when they think about military power. They assume that the strongest army always wins. That the country with the biggest budget, the most advanced weapons, the most aircraft carriers, that country will always come out on top. And on the surface, that logic makes total sense. Why wouldn't it? But here's where it gets interesting. And honestly, this idea flipped my entire understanding of how wars actually work. The United States has the most powerful military in the history of human civilization. I am not exaggerating when I say that. The budget alone is larger than the next 10 countries combined. The technology is generations ahead of most rivals. The global network of bases, the satellite intelligence systems, the nuclear arsenal, the carrier strike groups, it is genuinely unlike anything the world has ever seen before. If you put America in a direct conventional war against almost any other nation on earth, it is not...
