== Overview ==

The most dangerous moment in a bad war is not the day it starts. It is the day the strongest country in the war realizes it wants out. Because that is the moment fantasy collides with structure. That is the moment leaders discover something they almost never admit at the beginning. Starting a war and escaping a war are two completely different skills. You can launch missiles by choice. You can deploy troops by choice. You can sell the public a limited operation by choice. But once the war begins rearranging money, trade, alliances, prestige, and regional power, leaving is no longer just a military decision. It becomes a systemwide crisis. And that is the trap I want to talk about today. A lot of people still think war works like a switch. Go in, do damage, decide it is not worth it, go home, reset, move on.
